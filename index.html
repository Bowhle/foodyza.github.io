Search by Foodtruck

Find your go-to food truck in a snap! Whether you know exactly who you’re looking for, or you’re in the mood for a specific vendor, Foodyza lets you quickly locate them by name. See their location, menu, and more.

Search by Cuisine

Search by Location

Wherever you are, Foodyza helps you find the closest food trucks. Whether you’re at home, at work, or exploring a new area, quickly discover the delicious options around you. Perfect for those spontaneous foodie moments!

About Foodyza

Our Story

Foodyza is a passion project born from a simple idea: to make it easier for people to find and enjoy great food from local food trucks. Originally conceptualised in 2020 by Samantha Mkwananzi during her UI studies at CarlArts, the project was brought to life during her studies at ALX, in collaboration with Kgotso Mphuthi. Our web application is designed to connect users with the vibrant food truck scene in their area. Whether you’re a busy professional looking for a quick lunch, a student on a budget, or simply a foodie eager to explore new flavours, Foodyza is here to help.



The code for this project is available on GitHub.

Timeline

Here's how our journey began

  • 2020

    The Idea Takes Root

    A lightbulb moment sparked: a simple idea for a web application that connects foodies with local food trucks.

  • February 2025

    Initial Planning and Research

    Foodyza project research phase: defined team roles, technical choices, problem statement, target users, locale, risks, development process, and market analysis.

  • March 2025

    Foodyza MVP specification

    We worked on the initial Foodyza MVP specification outlining system architecture, API routes for user, food truck, menu, order, payment, and location management, 3rd party API integrations (Google Maps, PayFast), data model considerations, user stories, and UI mockups.

  • March 2025

    MVP built, Launched and presented

    Week 2 MVP progress report: 7/10 progress, core features implemented, integration in progress, visual design and some functionality complete, significant technical challenges with dependency management and port issues, non-technical challenges with time management.
